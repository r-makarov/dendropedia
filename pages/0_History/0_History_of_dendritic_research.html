History of dendritic research

Author: Roman Makarov

Date:

Dendritic research has significantly advanced our understanding of neuronal function. Early studies by Hodgkin and Huxley laid the groundwork with their model of action potentials. Subsequent research by Rall and others explored voltage attenuation and dendritic spikes, revealing the complex electrical properties of dendrites. These discoveries have been crucial in understanding how neurons process and integrate information, highlighting the importance of dendrites in neural computation.
